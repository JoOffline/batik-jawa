Batik Tuban

Tuban batik or also known as gedog batik is a typical batik from the Tuban people which is made by weaving, this batik motif is in the form of black and white squares or stripes. Tuban batik is called gedog batik because in the manufacturing process the cotton is spun into thread and then woven into cloth using a manual tool that makes a 'dog dog' sound. From that sound, the people of Tuban named the batik gedog batik. This batik is made in Kerek District, Tuban Regency.

Here is some information about Tuban Batik:

1. Various Motifs: Tuban Batik has a variety of motifs, ranging from complex geometric motifs to motifs inspired by nature, plants, animals, mythology, history or everyday life. These motifs are often arranged with great detail and complexity.

2. Traditional Making Process : The process of making batik usually takes 3 months for the spinning, weaving, batik and coloring processes. Tuban batik also involves traditional techniques, where hot wax is applied to the cloth using a tool called a canting to create the desired motifs. This technique allows the creation of complex motifs with a high level of detail.

3. Use of Specific Colors: The use of colors in gedog batik itself uses natural colors from indigo trees, noni trees and mango tree roots.

4. Closed Color Palette : Tuban Batik tends to use more neutral or closed colors, such as brown, dark green, dark blue, giving this batik work a classic impression.

5. Relationship with Local History and Culture: Tuban Batik motifs often contain philosophical, historical meaning, or are related to local wisdom and the lives of the Tuban people.

Use in Fashion and Art: Apart from being used in traditional clothing, Tuban Batik is also used in modern clothing and is part of local arts and crafts in Tuban.

People usually make batik while they are waiting for the planting period. The dot motif on gedog batik symbolizes Javanese-Hindu cosmology, namely the Qibla Papat Lima Pancer. The unique motifs and cultural values make a major contribution to the diversity of Indonesian culture and make Tuban Batik a symbol of the richness of Indonesian traditional art.

Cirebon, Pekalongan, and Lasem

Batik Pesisir

Pesisiran Batik, which reflects the influence of Chinese and Arab culture, displays typical variations in various regions. For example, Cirebon is famous With its colorful geometric motifs, Pekalongan is known for its motifs which is more diverse and continues to develop, while Lasem stands out with its motifs which has bright colors and a classic feel.

Yogyakarta and Surakarta (Solo)

Batik Jogja

Batik from this area has a more classic and traditional Javanese style. Yogyakarta Batik tends to have more subtle and elegant motifs, while batik from Solo tends to be bolder and stronger in its motifs. Yogyakarta Motif often featuring subtle nuances and intricate details, while Solo motifs feature prominently Bold lines and striking colors.

Madura

Batik Madura

This batik is distinctive in its geometric motifs and intricate carvings and bright colors, reflects the rich culture and local traditions of Madura. Madurese Batik motifs often appear a combination of bold and bright colors such as red, yellow and blue, which symbolize courage and the joy of the Madurese people, as well as displaying unique elements of nature and daily life.

Tuban

Batik Tuban

This batik is unique in its geometric motifs and complex carvings brightly colored, typical of Tuban which is famous for its unique design. Batik Motif Tuban often depicts local flora and fauna, as well as local folklore, which displayed in bright and attractive color compositions.

Banyumas

Batik Banyumas

Batik from Banyumas is known for its simpler motifs with soft and natural colors, reflects the beauty and simplicity of the natural surroundings. Banyumas batik motifs often display patterns which is inspired by rural environments, such as views of rice fields and mountains, combined with warm and natural colors.
